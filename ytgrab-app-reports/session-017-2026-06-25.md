## Summary

Low-risk redeploy bringing `app` from web/api **1.2.0** to **1.3.0** — the
**"proxy gate: protect healthy proxies, deterministic rotation, self-heal"** change.
1.2.0 (session-016) made the gate less aggressive; production data then showed *proven*
residential proxies (300+ uses, <2% fail) still being `recycled` on a single bad window —
expensive (burns Webshare replacement quota, 7-day IP denylist) and indiscriminate
(can't tell "bad proxy" from "bad video/YouTube"). 1.3.0 spares a working proxy: one that
succeeded within `PROXY_PROVEN_WINDOW_MS` (1h) is **never recycled** on a strike (only
disabled); each success **decays one strike**; faults carry **severity** (hard `407`/conn
reset can recycle; soft `403`/`429` byte-fetch never recycles an ever-worked proxy).
Selection switches from uniform-random to **deterministic LRU round-robin** (monotonic
counter, concurrency-safe), briefly skipping a just-faulted proxy (`PROXY_FAULT_SKIP_MS`);
residential disabled proxies re-try after a shorter `PROXY_DISABLE_COOLDOWN_RESIDENTIAL_MS`
(30m vs the datacenter 1-day). A **background self-heal** timer (`PROXY_REFRESH_INTERVAL_MS`,
30m) re-pulls the Webshare lists so the pool archives dropped proxies (**now including
recycled-but-gone**), revives reappeared ones, and prunes the denylist — no admin click.
