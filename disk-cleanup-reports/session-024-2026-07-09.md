# Session 24 — Emergency: Container Near-Full, Orphaned Ollama Models Found on `klo` Account

**Date:** 2026-07-09
**Disk before:** 100% used — 169MB free (container-level; df showed 487MB-541MB depending on the moment)
**Disk after:** 91% used — 19GB free
**Recovered:** ~20GB

---

## Context

User reported disk "almost 0" the morning after S23 (which had left 27GB free). The regular repeat-offender sweep (gradle/DerivedData/CoreSimulator/npx — the S23 playbook) only accounted for ~2GB of the gap, and `~/Users/dedi` itself (81-91GB) didn't explain the total 206GB used on the Data volume. This forced a full-volume investigation rather than the usual cache sweep.

**Root cause: this Mac has a second macOS user account, `klo`, not previously tracked in any disk-cleanup session.** `klo` is described by the user as their own automation/agent account (runs `code-server` + `ttyd` + an "openclaw" agent workspace). It was using 23GB, of which **14GB was a fully orphaned Ollama installation** — the `ollama` binary was gone from `/opt/homebrew/bin` (`command not found`) but `~/.ollama/models/blobs` still held two ~7GB model blobs, both last touched in April (3 months stale). The app had clearly been uninstalled without ever clearing its data.

### Volume-level investigation (why the usual sweep wasn't enough)

`du` on `/Users/dedi` alone (~91GB) couldn't explain 206GB used on the 245GB Data volume. Had to `sudo du -h -d1 /System/Volumes/Data` to get an authoritative breakdown, which surfaced `/Users` at 108GB total — `dedi` (81-91GB) + `klo` (23GB, previously invisible to non-sudo `du` due to permission walls) + `Shared` (1.9GB) + `awinta` (803MB, another dormant account, not reviewed).

Going forward, an early step in any "disk almost full" investigation should be `sudo du -h -d1 /System/Volumes/Data` to catch cross-account or system-level growth before assuming it's the usual `dedi`-side repeat offenders.
